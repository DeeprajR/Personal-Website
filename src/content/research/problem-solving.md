## The Problem-Solving Framework

### 1. Define the Problem
- What is the problem?
- Why is it a problem?
- Who is affected by it?
- What are the consequences of not solving it?

### 2. Analyze the Problem
- What are the root causes of the problem?
- What are the contributing factors?
- What are the constraints?
- What are the opportunities?

### 3. Generate Solutions
- What are the possible solutions?
- What are the pros and cons of each solution?
- What are the risks and benefits of each solution?
- What are the costs and benefits of each solution?

### 4. Select the Best Solution
- Which solution best addresses the problem?
- Which solution is most feasible?
- Which solution is most cost-effective?
- Which solution is most sustainable?

### 5. Implement the Solution
- What are the steps to implement the solution?
- What resources are needed?
- What is the timeline?
- Who is responsible for each step?

### 6. Monitor and Evaluate
- How will we measure success?
- How will we track progress?
- How will we make adjustments?
- How will we ensure sustainability?
